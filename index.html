1. Sri Digambar Jain Lal Mandir

What's in this picture?

This photo captures the stunning red towers of the Sri Digambar Jain Lal Mandir in Old Delhi. What really made the shot for me was that flock of pigeons gracefully circling the spires against that perfectly clear sky.

Why I took this photo:

I wanted to capture that unique blend of timeless tradition and vibrant life. The temple's ancient architecture speaks of spiritual heritage, but those birds in flight add such a sense of freedom and continuous movement. It feels like a beautiful conversation between history and the living present.

How I got the shot:

  • Composition: I aimed for a subtle Rule of Thirds here. The two main temple spires sit nicely near the vertical lines of the frame, creating balance. The birds were a bonus, adding a dynamic element across the top.
  • Lighting: It was natural daylight, probably mid-morning. The light was soft and diffused, which really helped illuminate the temple evenly without harsh shadows, preserving those rich red details.
  • Contrast: The contrast is moderate, which makes the temple's red pop beautifully against the lighter, almost sepia-toned sky. The birds provided sharp silhouettes.
  • ISO: With all that natural light, I kept the ISO low (around 100 or 200) to ensure maximum detail and keep noise to a minimum.
